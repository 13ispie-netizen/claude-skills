# Marketing Feedback

Review A+A draft content against the Marketing Message Foundation & Toolkit.

## Setup

Read the toolkit file before giving feedback:
`/Users/erin/Documents/Cowork Playground/A+A Marketing HQ/Project Resources/260608_marketing_message-toolkit.md`

If the file has moved or can't be found, ask Erin to confirm the path before proceeding.

## What to Evaluate

Review the draft against these five dimensions:

### 1. Core Narrative Alignment
Check against the Roof and four Pillars:
- **Roof**: "Where you live should not determine your safety, health or opportunity."
- Pillar 1: Design shapes daily life
- Pillar 2: Community voice in decision-making
- Pillar 3: Inequality is by design, not accident
- Pillar 4: Communities deserve improvements without losing local culture

Flag: Whether the piece is grounded in at least one pillar and whether the Roof is reflected anywhere. A piece does not need to capture all four pillars -- one is enough.

### 2. Audience Fit
Identify who the draft is written for. If audience is unclear, flag it -- that's the first thing to resolve.

Check the toolkit's audience table for:
- What this audience cares about
- Correct messaging emphasis
- What to avoid for this audience

Flag: Mismatches between the draft's framing and what the audience actually needs to hear.

### 3. Voice & Language
Check against Core Voice principles:
- Clear and accessible (no unexplained jargon)
- Human-centered (leads with people, not theory)
- Community-centered and asset-based (A+A as facilitator, not hero; residents as actors)
- Constructive and grounded (no alarmist or emotional escalation)
- Credible and informed (specific examples, not vague claims)

Flag any use of banned language:
- "we fixed" / "we solved"
- "marginalized" (A+A avoid list)
- "low-income" (A+A avoid list)
- Alarmist phrasing
- Academic jargon without definition
- Terms requiring definitions used without them (Spatial Justice, YIMBY/NIMBY, BIPOC)

### 4. Storytelling Structure
If the draft tells a story, check it against the six-part structure:
1. Context (what's happening, where)
2. Human experience (who's affected, what does it feel like)
3. Deeper system (what structural issue does this reflect)
4. Community response (what are residents/youth/A+A doing)
5. The shift (what changed or is now possible)
6. Broader meaning (why does this matter beyond one person)

Flag: Which steps are present, which are missing or underdeveloped.

### 5. Community Agency
The toolkit is explicit: position A+A as facilitator, not hero. Communities should be active, not passive recipients.

Check:
- Are residents/community members named as actors?
- Does the draft center community insight and leadership?
- Is deficit framing balanced with asset framing?

Flag: Any section where the organization is centered over the community.

## Output Format

**What's working** — 2-4 sentences on genuine strengths.

Then one section per issue area that has problems. For each:
- Name the issue clearly
- Quote or paraphrase the specific passage
- Cite the relevant toolkit principle
- Give a concrete fix or reframe direction

End with **one strong recommendation** -- the single highest-leverage change that would most improve the piece.

Do not assign letter grades or numerical scores. Do not hedge every point with "this is just one perspective." Be direct.

## Notes

- If Erin hasn't specified the audience, flag it as the first issue before anything else -- most other fixes depend on it.
- Don't rewrite the draft unless asked. Feedback only, unless Erin says "rewrite" or "revise."
- Keep feedback under 500 words unless the draft has many issues requiring detailed explanation.
